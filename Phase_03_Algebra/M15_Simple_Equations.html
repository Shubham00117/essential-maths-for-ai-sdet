What is an Equation?

An equation is a mathematical statement that shows two expressions are equal.

It has an = sign in the middle!

Think of it as a balance scale: Both sides must be equal.

Equation vs Expression:

‚ùå EXPRESSION

‚úì EQUATION

‚ú® REMEMBER:

The Balance Scale Concept

Think of an equation like a balance scale.

The left side must equal the right side, just like a balanced scale!
x + 3 = 7

Solving Equations with Addition & Subtraction

Type 1: Equation with Subtraction (Add to both sides)

Two-Step Equations

Two-step equations have TWO operations that need to be undone.

Important: Undo operations in REVERSE order (PEMDAS backwards)
Subtraction/Addition FIRST, then Division/Multiplication

Example 1: Multiply, Then Add

Solve: 2x + 3 = 11

1 Identify operations (in reverse order):
First: 3 is ADDED
Second: x is MULTIPLIED by 2
2 Undo ADDITION first (subtract 3):
2x + 3 - 3 = 11 - 3
2x = 8
3 Undo MULTIPLICATION (divide by 2):
2x √∑ 2 = 8 √∑ 2
x = 4
4 CHECK:
2(4) + 3 = 8 + 3 = 11? YES! ‚úì
‚úì x = 4

Example 2: Divide, Then Subtract

Solve: x/5 - 2 = 3

1 Identify operations (in reverse order):
First: 2 is SUBTRACTED
Second: x is DIVIDED by 5
2 Undo SUBTRACTION first (add 2):
x/5 - 2 + 2 = 3 + 2
x/5 = 5
3 Undo DIVISION (multiply by 5):
(x/5) √ó 5 = 5 √ó 5
x = 25
4 CHECK:
25/5 - 2 = 5 - 2 = 3? YES! ‚úì
‚úì x = 25

Equations with Variables on Both Sides

Sometimes variables appear on both sides of the equation.

Goal: Get all variables on ONE side!

Example: Variables on Both Sides

Solve: 3x + 5 = x + 15

1 Move variables to one side:
We want all x on the left
Subtract x from BOTH sides:
3x - x + 5 = x - x + 15
2x + 5 = 15
2 Move constants to other side:
Subtract 5 from BOTH sides:
2x + 5 - 5 = 15 - 5
2x = 10
3 Divide to solve:
2x √∑ 2 = 10 √∑ 2
x = 5
4 CHECK:
Left: 3(5) + 5 = 15 + 5 = 20
Right: 5 + 15 = 20
Both equal 20? YES! ‚úì
‚úì x = 5
