What is a Ratio?

A ratio is a way to compare two or more quantities. It tells you how much of one thing there is compared to another.

Think of it as: "For every X of this, there is Y of that"

How to Write a Ratio

There are 3 ways to write a ratio:
Method 1: Using Colon (:)

(Read as "3 to 2")
Method 2: Using the Word "to"

3 to 2
Method 3: As a Fraction

3/2
‚ú® All three mean the same thing:
3:2 = 3 to 2 = 3/2

Equivalent Ratios

Just like with fractions, ratios can be equivalent (mean the same thing).

If you multiply or divide both parts of a ratio by the same number, you get an equivalent ratio.

Example: Making Equivalent Ratios

Starting Ratio: 2:3

2 : 3
1 Multiply both by 2:
(2 √ó 2) : (3 √ó 2) = 4 : 6
These are equivalent! 2:3 = 4:6
2 Multiply both by 3:
(2 √ó 3) : (3 √ó 3) = 6 : 9
These are equivalent! 2:3 = 6:9
3 Divide both by 1 (if possible):
(2 √∑ 1) : (3 √∑ 1) = 2 : 3
This is the simplest form
‚ú® All of these are EQUIVALENT:
2:3 = 4:6 = 6:9 = 8:12 = 10:15

They all mean the same relationship!

Simplifying Ratios (Finding the Simplest Form)

To simplify a ratio, divide both parts by their GCD (Greatest Common Divisor).

Example 1: Simplify 6:9

1 Find the GCD of 6 and 9:
Factors of 6: 1, 2, 3, 6
Factors of 9: 1, 3, 9
GCD = 3
2 Divide both by 3:
(6 √∑ 3) : (9 √∑ 3) = 2 : 3
3 Check: Can we divide further? NO! ‚úì
2 and 3 have no common factors (except 1)
‚úì Answer: 6:9 simplifies to 2:3

Example 2: Simplify 12:18

1 Find the GCD of 12 and 18:
Factors of 12: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12
Factors of 18: 1, 2, 3, 6, 9, 18
GCD = 6
2 Divide both by 6:
(12 √∑ 6) : (18 √∑ 6) = 2 : 3
‚úì Answer: 12:18 simplifies to 2:3
