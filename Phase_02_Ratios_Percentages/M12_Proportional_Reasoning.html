What is Proportional Reasoning?

Proportional reasoning is understanding and using the relationship between quantities that change together in a consistent way.

In simple terms: When one quantity increases, the other increases in the SAME ratio (or pattern).

Key Idea: The ratio between two quantities STAYS THE SAME

Real-World Examples:

üçî Recipe Scaling

Recipe calls for 2 cups flour
If you double the recipe, you need 4 cups

If you triple it, you need 6 cups

Pattern: 2 ‚Üí 4 ‚Üí 6
(Always multiply by same factor)
üöó Speed & Distance

Drive 60 mph for 1 hour = 60 miles
Drive 60 mph for 2 hours = 120 miles
Drive 60 mph for 3 hours = 180 miles

Double time = Double distance
‚ú® CORE IDEA:
If one quantity doubles, triples, or halves,
the other quantity does the SAME!

Proportions: Comparing Ratios

A proportion is a statement that two ratios are equal.

Written as: a/b = c/d (or a:b = c:d)

This means the ratios are equivalent - they have the same relationship!

Direct vs Inverse Proportions

When one quantity increases, the other also increases in the same ratio.

Formula: y = kx (where k is a constant)

When one quantity increases, the other decreases in the opposite ratio.

Formula: y = k/x (where k is a constant)

(Inverse)
üë• Workers & Time

Unit Rates (Part of Proportional Reasoning)

A unit rate compares a quantity to ONE unit of another quantity.

Examples: price per item, miles per hour, cost per pound

üçé Example: 6 apples cost $12. What's the unit rate (price per apple)?

1 Divide total by quantity:

‚úì Unit Rate: $2 per apple

2 Multiply unit rate by quantity:

‚úì 8 apples cost $16
