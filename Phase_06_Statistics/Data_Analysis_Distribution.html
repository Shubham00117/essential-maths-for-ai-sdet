What is Data Distribution?

Distribution describes how data values are spread or arranged.

It shows the frequency of each value and the overall pattern of the data.
üìä Shape How the data looks when graphed
(symmetric, skewed, etc.)
üìç Center Where most values cluster
(mean, median, mode)
üìè Spread How spread out values are
(range, std deviation)

Types of Distributions

1. Normal Distribution (Bell Curve)

The most common distribution. Data clusters around the mean, with fewer values at extremes.
Symmetric - Mean = Median = Mode
‚ú® 68-95-99.7 Rule (Empirical Rule):
‚Ä¢ 68% of data falls within 1 standard deviation of mean
‚Ä¢ 95% of data falls within 2 standard deviations
‚Ä¢ 99.7% of data falls within 3 standard deviations

2. Skewed Distributions

Left Skewed (Negative)

Tail extends to the LEFT
Mean < Median < Mode

Example: Easy test scores
(most score high)

Right Skewed (Positive)

Tail extends to the RIGHT
Mode < Median < Mean

Example: Income distribution
(few earn very high)

3. Uniform Distribution

All values have equal probability.
Example: Rolling a fair die (each outcome 1-6 has equal chance)

Quartiles & Percentiles

Quartiles divide data into 4 equal parts.
Percentiles show the percentage of values below a given point.

The Five-Number Summary:

Measure Symbol Meaning
Minimum Min Smallest value
First Quartile Q1 25th percentile (25% below)
Median Q2 50th percentile (middle)
Third Quartile Q3 75th percentile (75% below)
Maximum Max Largest value

üìù Example: Find Quartiles

Data (sorted): 2, 4, 6, 8, 10, 12, 14, 16
1Find Median (Q2):
Middle of 8 values = (8 + 10) √∑ 2 = 9
2Find Q1:
Median of lower half (2,4,6,8) = (4+6) √∑ 2 = 5
3Find Q3:
Median of upper half (10,12,14,16) = (12+14) √∑ 2 = 13
Min=2 | Q1=5 | Q2=9 | Q3=13 | Max=16

Interquartile Range (IQR)

IQR = Q3 - Q1

Measures the spread of the middle 50% of data
From example above: IQR = 13 - 5 = 8

Outliers

Outliers are values that are unusually far from other values.
They can significantly affect mean and other calculations.
Lower Fence = Q1 - 1.5 √ó IQR
Upper Fence = Q3 + 1.5 √ó IQR

Values outside these fences are outliers!

üìù Example: Find Outliers

Given: Q1 = 5, Q3 = 13, IQR = 8
1Lower Fence:
5 - 1.5 √ó 8 = 5 - 12 = -7
2Upper Fence:
13 + 1.5 √ó 8 = 13 + 12 = 25
Any value < -7 or> 25 is an outlier

Correlation

Correlation measures the relationship between two variables.
Ranges from -1 to +1
Positive (+1) Both increase together

Example: Height & Weight
Zero (0) No relationship

Example: Shoe size & IQ
Negative (-1) One increases, other decreases

Example: Speed & Travel time
üí° Remember: Correlation ‚â† Causation!
Just because two things are related doesn't mean one causes the other.
