What is Spread?

Spread (or Dispersion) measures how spread out the data values are.

Central tendency tells us the center; Spread tells us how far values are from that center.
Low Spread Data points are close together

Example: 48, 49, 50, 51, 52
(clustered around 50)
High Spread Data points are far apart

Example: 10, 30, 50, 70, 90
(widely scattered)
